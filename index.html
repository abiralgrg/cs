My attitude to learning

Efficiency

I believe that many students don’t struggle because they lack ability, but because their effort isn’t always focused in the right direction. In my own experience, a lot of time was wasted covering content that never came up in exams, especially when lessons didn’t align closely with what exam boards actually required. That’s why I place such a strong emphasis on efficient learning: using subject specifications to guide revision and ensuring that every minute spent studying is moving you closer to exam success.

Improvement

My biggest progress came when I shifted my focus to what actually helps you improve not just working harder, but working smarter. I started targeting my weaker areas directly, using mark schemes, examiners’ reports, and past papers to guide my practice. I learned that the best results come from refining exam technique and being strategic, not from trying to memorise everything in a textbook. Improvement comes from knowing where to focus and I can help students do exactly that.
